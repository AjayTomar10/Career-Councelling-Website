Must visit Educational Platforms

The National Digital Library of India is more than just a collection of learning resources. It's a virtual repository offering textbooks, articles, videos, audio books, lectures, simulations, fiction, and more for learners. This project, under the Ministry of Education, Government of India, aims to gather metadata and full-text content from national and international digital libraries and other relevant sources. NDLI offers free access to a wide range of books, supports multiple languages, and is managed by the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur.

DIKSHA, short for Digital Infrastructure for Knowledge Sharing, is a nationwide platform dedicated to enhancing school education. Led by the NCERT (National Council for Educational Research and Training), it serves as a digital hub where schools, teachers, content partners, NGOs, and corporations collaborate to offer a diverse range of educational resources, supporting teaching and learning in schools. Contributors include schools, teachers, content partners, non-profits, and CSR-focused companies, creating a valuable collection of materials aligned with NCERT, CBSE, and state educational board curricula

The National Institute of Open Schooling (NIOS), formerly known as the National Open School (NOS), was established in November 1989 as an autonomous organization under the Ministry of Education, Government of India. NIOS offers a variety of vocational, life enrichment, and community-oriented courses in addition to general and academic courses at the secondary and senior secondary levels. It also provides elementary-level courses through its Open Basic Education Programmes (OBE). Through a government notification, NIOS has been authorized to conduct examinations and certify learners up to the pre-degree level, whether in academic, technical, or vocational fields. The Association of Indian Universities has also granted equivalence to NIOS's Senior Secondary Certificate Examination.

SWAYAM is a government initiative in India focused on providing equitable access to high-quality education. It aims to bridge the digital divide by offering interactive courses, from Class 9 to post-graduation, for free to all learners. These courses are created by top educators and are accessible anytime, anywhere. Over 1,000 faculty members and teachers from across the country have contributed to this effort.

NPTEL, a collaboration between IITs and IISc, funded by the Ministry of Education, Government of India, started in 2003. It began as an initiative to provide quality education across the country and
now offers nearly 600+ certification courses each semester in 22 different disciplines.

It also provides digital textbooks, graded learning materials, and opportunities for participation in various educational events.
